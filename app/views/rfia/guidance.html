Help for creating RFIA's

An RFIA is required if a project or change has a big impact upon the business or requires many resources to complete. For example you would create an RFIA to move a large project team and it's resources from one building to another or to equip a large project team with expensive software licences.

The RFIA process is not necessary for small projects or changes. For example, you wouldnt create an RFIA for one person to be issued a laptop. You may not even need an RFIA to create a small service that will be used by only a handful of staff in one building.

The description should cover three main areas:

  • The background of the request
  • The specific requirements of the request
  • The reason the change is required

It's important to be as specific as possible. For example, in the requirements you will need to include the hardware and/or software licences that you know to be required and in what quantity.

CON delivery refers to when you require the contractual agreement should start

'latest business critical deadline' refers to the very latest that the contractual agreement can start on

All dates are aspirational and may be affected by other requests being processed by Capita. It may take up to 21 days for a project to get to CON delivery

Disaster recovery and business continuity plans set out the processes that are followed to recover critical services that have been affected by disasters or by major business changes. Your request will have implications on these plans if it affects how they will need to be performed

If the request will affect how data and/or information is stored, handled or processed then it will be affected by GDPR. You should ensure that the GDPR team is informed of the request
